Day 2: Organizational Design I

4. Organizational Differentiation

Differentiation: Division of labor in an organization into specialized roles and units.

  • Benefits: Specialization increases efficiency, expertise, and focus.
  • Challenges: Coordination and communication problems between units.
  • Vertical Differentiation: Layers of management (hierarchy).
  • Horizontal Differentiation: Functional departments (e.g., marketing, IT).

5. Mintzberg's Five Coordination Mechanisms

  • Mutual Adjustment: Informal communication (startups, creative teams).
  • Direct Supervision: One person issues instructions (small businesses).
  • Standardization of Work Processes: Defined processes and procedures (factories).
  • Standardization of Outputs: Defined goals regardless of method (sales teams).
  • Standardization of Skills: Employees trained similarly (hospitals, universities).

6. Integration to Overcome Differentiation Problems

Integration Mechanisms: Tools to improve coordination across differentiated units.

  • Hierarchy: Managers resolve issues across departments.
  • Liaison Roles: People designated to link departments.
  • Task Forces: Temporary teams for specific projects.
  • Cross-functional Teams: Members from various departments working together.
