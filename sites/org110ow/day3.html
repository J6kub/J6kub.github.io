Day 3: Organizational Design II

7. Centralization vs Decentralization

  • Centralization: Decision-making at the top. Efficient but slow and less flexible.
  • Decentralization: Empowering lower levels. More responsive and innovative, but risks inconsistency.

8. Mechanistic vs Organic Structures

Mechanistic: Rigid, hierarchical, suitable for stable environments (e.g., manufacturing).

Organic: Flexible, collaborative, ideal for dynamic settings (e.g., tech startups).

9. Minimum Chain of Command

Principle: Use the fewest levels of authority needed. Impacts structure height and span of control (how many report to one supervisor).

10. Coping with Chain of Command & Span of Control

Balance is key: Too tall = slow; too wide = overloaded managers. Solution: delegate, use IT tools, train middle managers.
