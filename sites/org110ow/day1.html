Day 1: Organizations and Their Environment

1. Natural vs Rational Perspectives

Rational Perspective: Views organizations as goal-oriented, structured systems designed to achieve specific ends through formal rules and hierarchy (like a machine).

Natural Perspective: Sees organizations as organic, living systems with informal networks, internal politics, and a focus on survival rather than just efficiency.

Example: A software company may have a formal hierarchy (rational), but team collaboration and social norms (natural) often dictate how work gets done.

2. Resource Dependency Theory (RDT)

Main Idea: Organizations depend on external resources and must manage relationships to reduce uncertainty and dependence.

  • Core Concepts: Power, dependence, strategic alliances
  • Application: Forming joint ventures or lobbying to manage external pressures

Example: An IT firm relying on a cloud provider may diversify providers or invest in its own infrastructure to reduce dependency.

3. Transaction Cost Economics (TCE)

Main Concern: Organizations seek to minimize the cost of transactions (e.g., negotiating, contracting, monitoring).

  • Key Concepts: Asset specificity, bounded rationality, opportunism
  • Make vs Buy: Firms internalize operations when external markets are too costly or risky.

Example: A company may hire in-house developers (make) instead of outsourcing (buy) to avoid coordination problems and protect proprietary knowledge.
