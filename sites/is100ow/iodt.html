Impact of Digital Technologies Overview

Impact of Digital Technologies

Digital technologies can promote equality, peace, and justice by supporting the Sustainable Development Goals, but they also pose risks to privacy, security, and equality. Managing these technologies requires careful choices.

Rapid Advancement

Digital technologies have rapidly transformed societies, enhancing connectivity and access to services, acting as equalizers.

Health and Education

AI and digital technologies save lives, diagnose diseases, extend life expectancy, and make education and public services more accessible and efficient.

Connectivity Gaps

Many remain disconnected, with women, the elderly, and marginalized groups being disproportionately affected. Connectivity gaps persist, especially in the least developed countries.

Algorithmic Bias

Algorithms can perpetuate biases if based on non-diverse data, and the lack of diversity in tech exacerbates this issue.

Labor Force Changes

Technological revolutions alter the labor force, creating new jobs and rendering others obsolete. The green economy could create millions of jobs, but automation might displace many workers.

Skill Gaps

Managing technological changes requires a focus on STEM education, soft skills, and lifelong learning. Unpaid work also needs better support.

Human Rights and Privacy

Digital technologies can defend or violate human rights, depending on their use. Data exploitation is a growing concern.

Data Ownership

Better regulation of personal data ownership could empower individuals and promote welfare and rights.

Social Media

Social media connects people globally, but it can also spread hate speech and misinformation, creating social fragmentation.

Geopolitical Tensions

Digital developments are debated amidst rising geopolitical tensions. The UN advocates for digital cooperation to avoid a fragmented digital world.
