Evolution of Industry

Industry 1.0 - Steam Powered Machines

Industry 1.0, also known as the First Industrial Revolution, began in the late 18th century and was characterized by the use of steam power and mechanization of production. This era saw the introduction of steam engines and machines that greatly increased production capabilities and efficiency.

Key Developments:

  • Steam engines
  • Textile machinery
  • Mechanized factories

Industry 2.0 - Electrical Machines

Industry 2.0, or the Second Industrial Revolution, occurred in the late 19th and early 20th centuries. This period was marked by the widespread adoption of electricity, which powered new innovations and production methods. The assembly line and mass production became prominent features of this era.

Key Developments:

  • Electricity
  • Assembly lines
  • Mass production

Industry 3.0 - Computers

Industry 3.0, also known as the Third Industrial Revolution, began in the mid-20th century and was driven by the advent of computers and automation. This era saw the rise of digital technology, programmable logic controllers (PLCs), and significant advancements in automation and information technology.

Key Developments:

  • Computers
  • Automation
  • Information technology

Industry 4.0 - Cyber-Physical Systems

Industry 4.0, or the Fourth Industrial Revolution, is the current era of industrial development characterized by the integration of cyber-physical systems, the Internet of Things (IoT), and smart automation. This period emphasizes the use of connected devices, data exchange, and advanced technologies to create intelligent, self-optimizing systems.

Key Developments:

  • Cyber-physical systems
  • Internet of Things (IoT)
  • Smart automation
